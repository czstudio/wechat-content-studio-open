# Workflow Playbook

## 1. Topic Board

Use topic cards instead of a cold ranked list.

The card should show:

- who the article is for
- what pain or curiosity it hits
- what opening can pull the reader in
- what evidence already exists
- whether it is ready now

## 2. Draft Brief

Use when a winner is clear.

The brief must answer:

- what promise this article makes
- what opening to use
- what 3-part spine to follow
- what material must be used
- what not to drift into

## 3. Draft

The draft should:

- start with a scene, question, contradiction, or failure
- move through one stable spine
- use real support where trust is needed
- remain mobile-readable

## 4. Proof Gate

Review before polish.

Check:

- support chain: opening / middle / ending
- AI flavor
- title-body alignment
- publish readiness

## 5. Repair Brief

If the draft is weak but salvageable, do not just say “rewrite”.

Return:

- primary failure
- support matrix
- 1-3 must-fix items
- section or paragraph targets

## 6. Image Plan

Only after the argument is stable.

Separate:

- cover
- inline explanation image
- infographic
- cross-platform pack

## 7. Publish Prep

Only after:

- no fatal factual issue
- no unresolved structural weakness
- layout and visuals are safe to continue
